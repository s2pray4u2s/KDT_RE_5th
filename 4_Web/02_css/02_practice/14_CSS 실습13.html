The Kid LAROI, Justine Bieber - STAY (novel ver.)

He always found himself frozen in the same kind of night.
The city was loud, but his room was quiet—too quiet.
His phone lay face down beside him, as if it could still feel her presence.
Since she left, nothing felt stable.
He tried to drown the silence with noise, laughter, and strangers’ voices,
but the moment he was alone, her absence grew heavier.

He knew the truth, even if he hated it.
He wasn’t good at holding onto love.
He spoke before thinking, ran before understanding,
and mistook fear for affection.

She once told him he loved her hardest when he was afraid of losing her.
At the time, he denied it.
Now, the words echoed endlessly in his mind.

He made a thousand promises in his head—
that he would change,
that he would be calmer,
that he would learn how to love without hurting.

But promises meant nothing if she never came back to hear them.

So he waited.
Not with confidence, but with hope thin as glass.
Every night felt like tonight might be different,
like maybe this time she would return.

He didn’t ask for forgiveness anymore.
He didn’t ask for another chance.

He only wanted one thing—
that she would stay.
